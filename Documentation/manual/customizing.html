Customizing & Themes

Almost everything you see can be reshaped, recolored, and resized. It all happens in the Settings window — open it from the Toolbar's Settings button or by typing /myui settings.

Saving your changes

The Settings window has three buttons across the top:

ButtonWhat it does
Save SettingsSaves all your styling changes so they survive logout
RevertThrows away unsaved changes and goes back to your last save
Copy Settings…Opens the tool for copying your setup to another character
Styling changes apply instantly but aren't kept until you press Save. So you can freely experiment — drag sliders, try colors — and either keep the result with Save Settings or discard it with Revert (or simply by logging out). Showing/hiding a window, locking a window, and choosing a theme are the exceptions: those are saved the moment you do them.

Editing another character's settings

At the top of the Settings window is an Editing dropdown — "Edit this character, or push changes to another box." Normally it reads This Character and you're editing your own setup. Open the dropdown to pick one of your other characters instead:

  • Characters currently logged in appear available to select; offline ones are dimmed but still selectable.
  • When you pick another character, the window title shows you're editing them (highlighted), and a reminder line appears: "Editing <name> (remote) — Save applies the changes to that character."
  • Make your changes, then press Save Settings to send them to that character. Revert discards them.
An online character applies your changes live and confirms them back. An offline character can't receive them yet, so the changes are stored on your side and sync to them the next time they log in.
When you save your own settings, any changes are also pushed automatically to your other logged-in characters running MyUI — so a tweak you make on one box can ripple out to the rest without opening the Copy tool.

The Settings tabs

TabWhat you can change
WindowsShow or hide each window, and lock it in place.
BarsStyle every progress bar (see below).
ScalingSet the text size and icon size for each window independently.
DisplayPer-window options — things like showing the target block, using an icon view for buffs, a vertical pet bar, or Group window color and slot display settings.
TogglesThe look of the on/off switches used throughout the interface (label position, hover glow, knob style, and more).
ThemePick an overall color theme, or open the Themes editor to make your own.

Turning animations on or off

The interface's buttons, switches, and highlights have subtle motion — a soft glow when you hover, a little "press" bounce when you click, and a gentle sheen. If you'd rather have a calmer, completely still interface, turn off Animated Widgets in the General settings. Everything keeps its colors and stays fully usable — it simply stops moving. (Progress bars aren't affected; they keep their own per-bar shimmer and glow settings.)

Editing a text value? Some settings show their value in a small framed box with a pencil icon. Click it and a little editor pops up at your mouse — type the new value and press Accept (or Enter), or Cancel (or Esc) to leave it unchanged. Search and filter boxes still work the usual way: just type, and the list filters as you go.

Styling your bars

The Bars tab is the heart of customization. On the left is a tree: pick a window, then one of its bars (for example Player → HP). On the right, an editor lets you change that bar in detail:

GroupWhat you control
GeometryHorizontal or vertical, height/width, corner rounding, end padding
Fill & GradientThe fill colors and an optional gradient (including diagonal directions), static or changing with the value
BorderAn optional outline, its thickness and color
TextWhat to show on the bar — nothing, a value, a percent, or current·max — its format, and its size
TicksOptional tick marks along the bar
ShimmerA moving sheen, with speed and width
GlowA soft glow around the bar
AnimationHow smoothly the bar slides to a new value

Two handy buttons sit alongside the editor: Copy this style to… applies the bar you just styled to another bar (so you can make all your bars match in one move), and Reset to default restores a bar to its starting look.

Bars start out plain — every fancy effect is off by default — so the interface looks clean until you decide to add flair. Turn on a gradient, rounding, and a subtle glow for a modern look, or keep it simple.

Group and Raid window display settings

The Display tab includes options for how the Group window (which also apply to the Raid window) shows your party or raid members:

OptionWhat it does
Out-of-Zone Bar ColorA color picker that sets the color of health/mana bars for members who are in a different zone from you — such as your other boxed characters. This makes them stand out at a glance. Default is grey.
Show Empty SlotsWhen enabled, the Group window draws a placeholder tile for each empty group slot, making it clear how many members you could add. When disabled, only filled slots appear.

Scaling text and icons

The Scaling tab lets you set the text size and icon size of each window on its own. Make the group window's text bigger for readability, or shrink the inventory icons to fit more on screen — your choice, per window.

Themes

A theme is the overall color scheme for the whole interface. On the Theme tab, pick a theme from the dropdown and it applies immediately. You can also type /myui theme set <name> to switch themes, or /myui theme list to see what's available.

The Themes editor

Want your own colors? Open the Themes editor (from the Theme tab or /myui theme). It lets you adjust colors and spacing with a live preview, then save your creation under a name so you can switch to it any time.

Copying a setup between characters

The Copy Settings… button opens a tool that copies a setup from one of your characters onto another:

  1. Choose a source character (the one you like) and one or more destination characters — Ctrl+click in the destination list to pick several at once.
  2. The tool lists the differences, grouped by window. Each difference shows the source value and the destination value side by side.
  3. Copy a single setting, a whole window's section, or use Copy All for everything. The copy goes to every destination you selected.

If a destination character is currently logged in, it updates right away — no need to log out and back in. This is the quick way to give all your characters the same look.
