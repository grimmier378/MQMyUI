Multi-Boxing

If you run several characters at once, MQMyUI links them together. Each of your boxed characters quietly shares its health, mana, buffs, experience, and tracked items with the others, so a single window can show your whole team — and let you act on any of them. There's nothing to configure: as long as MQMyUI is loaded on each character, they find each other. When a character logs in, it automatically pulls settings from the others so your windows and customizations mirror reliably across your entire team.

This sharing happens between your own running characters on the same setup. It doesn't require any other add-on, and it only involves your own team.

The Group window

The Group window shows your party's health, mana, endurance, and pets. Its standout feature is that it includes characters who are out of your zone, as long as they're your own boxes — so you can keep an eye on a character parked elsewhere. It also shows:

  • Role and line-of-sight icons — who's the tank, who's the assist, who can see the target.
  • Distance and movement — how far away each member is and whether they're moving or sitting.
  • Click to target — click a member to make them your target.

The Raid window

For larger groups, the Raid window lays out your whole raid as a compact grid. It keeps its own size and styling separate from the Group window, so you can tune each for its job.

XP Bars and AA allocation

The XP Bars window shows a tile for each character with their experience, AA experience, and breath/air. Each tile also has quick buttons to control how that character splits experience into AA:

ButtonWhat it does
MinSend no experience to AA
Less / MoreNudge the AA share down or up by one step
MidSend about half of experience to AA
MaxSend all experience to AA

Because these buttons reach across to each character, you can set everyone's AA split from one window without switching screens.

Acting on another character

Right-click a character in the group, raid, or other shared windows for a menu of actions that run on that character:

ActionWhat it does
Come to meHas that character navigate to you
Switch toBrings that character's game window to the foreground
Make leaderPasses group leadership
Remove buffRemoves a named buff from that character
Block buffStops a spell from landing on that character again

Shared buffs

The Buffs window can show your group members' buffs, not just your own, so you can confirm everyone has the buffs they should — useful before a fight or after a wipe-recovery rebuff.

Shared item tracking

The iTrack window's counts are shared across your team: add an item to the tracked list once and every character reports how many they're carrying. See Inventory & Items.

Copying a setup to another character

Once you've styled one character the way you like, you don't have to redo it on the others. The Copy tool (opened from the Settings window) copies a setup from one character onto another, and the receiving character updates immediately. See Customizing & Themes.

Streaming & privacy (anonymizer)

If you record or stream, turning on MacroQuest's anonymize hides every character name in MyUI — player, group, raid, target, buffs, XP/AA, inventory and item-tracking — replacing each with a safe code built from that character's race, class and level (for example HIE_WIZ_65). These codes are never valid character names, so a viewer can't copy a name off your video to harass or mass-report the player. Your own character still shows as "Me" so you can always pick yourself out at a glance. Real, in-game targets that aren't players (mobs, NPCs) keep their names. Turn it off again and real names return instantly.
