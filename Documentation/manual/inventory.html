Inventory & Items

MQMyUI gives you three ways to handle your gear: the Inventory window for your worn equipment and bags, the BigBag browser for searching and sorting everything at once, and iTrack for keeping an eye on items across your characters. All three open from the Toolbar.

The Inventory window

Inventory shows a paperdoll of your worn slots on the left and your bags on the right, with your coin at the bottom. It resizes itself to fit.

Handling items

Items respond to the mouse the same way throughout the interface:

ActionWhat happens
Left-clickPick the item up onto your cursor (or drop a held item)
Right-clickUse the item
Ctrl + Right-clickOpen the game's built-in item display for that item
Shift + Right-clickOpen MQMyUI's own detailed item info window
Hovering an item always shows a tooltip with its full details. The four hint lines at the bottom of the tooltip remind you of these click options.

Swapping equipment

Right-click one of your worn slots (with no modifier key) to open a swap menu. It lists every item in your bags that fits that slot, so you can change gear without digging through bags. To keep the list short and clear, if you're carrying several copies of an item with the same final stats, they show up as a single entry — and an augmented version of an item is listed separately from a plain one, so you can tell them apart. Hover an entry to compare its stats against what you have equipped.

Bag pop-outs

Right-click a bag in the bags area to pop it open as its own window. Each pop-out has a small lock icon — click it to lock that bag's window in place so it can't be moved or resized by accident. Press Esc while a pop-out is focused to close it.

The item info window

Shift + Right-click any item to open the detailed info window. It lays out the item's stats in neat aligned columns and adds things the standard display doesn't:

  • Augment sockets — every socket on the item, with the augment in it (if any). Click a socketed augment to open its own info window.
  • Spell effects — for clicky items, procs, and focus effects, the spell name is a clickable link and a plain-language description of what it does is shown right there.
  • Augmented totals — stat lines include the bonuses from your socketed augments, so the numbers match what you actually get.

Inserting an augment

You can socket an augment straight from the info window:

  1. Pick up the augment so it's on your cursor.
  2. Open the item info window for the item you want to augment (Shift + Right-click it).
  3. Empty sockets that the augment fits turn green. Click a green socket.
  4. A confirmation appears at your cursor — confirm it, and the augment is inserted. The window refreshes to show the new augment and updated stats.
The game decides whether an augment fits a socket, so only valid sockets light up green. If nothing turns green, that augment can't go in that item.

The BigBag browser

BigBag is for when you want to see everything at once. It has tabs for Items, Clickies, Augments, Bank, and item Details. You can search by name, sort by name, stack size, or type, and start bulk trades. It's the fastest way to find a particular item or clean house.

The Name, Stack, and Type sort checkboxes stack together in that priority: type first, then name, then stack size. With Type on, items are grouped by what they are — all your armor together, all your food together, each weapon type together — then alphabetically by name within each group, and finally larger stacks first. Turn off any checkbox to drop that level of sorting.

Tracking items across characters

The iTrack window keeps a shared list of items you care about and shows how many each of your characters is holding — handy for tracking down who has the key, the food, or the tradeskill component. Your boxes share these counts automatically. See Multi-Boxing for how sharing works.
